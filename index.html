Advancing Glaucoma Science

Published Research & Theory

Dr. Hasnain has dedicated decades to investigating the fundamental mechanisms of chronic glaucoma. His pioneering “Sinking & Severance Theory” proposes a paradigm shift in understanding how nerve fibers are lost — challenging conventional wisdom in the field.

His research argues that glaucoma may not be an optic neuropathy, but rather an optic axotomy — where the lamina cribrosa sinks in the scleral canal, leading to orderly severance of nerve fibers from peripheral to central.

PubMed · 2006

Scleral Edge, Not Optic Disc or Retina, Is the Primary Site of Injury in Chronic Glaucoma

Published in Medical Hypotheses. The foundational paper presenting the unconventional approach that the primary site of injury lies at the scleral edge, not the optic disc or retina.

Rethinking the Pathogenesis of Primary Open-Angle Glaucoma: An Optic Disc Axotomy

Co-authored with Aziz Hasnain. Presents the complete case that POAG may be an axotomy rather than a neuropathy, with the lamina cribrosa sinking in the scleral canal.

ResearchGate · 2013

Arcuate Field Defects in Glaucoma

A detailed exploration of how the orderly loss of arcuate nerve fibers produces the characteristic visual field defects that are the pathognomonic feature of glaucoma.

EC Ophthalmology · 2020

Arcuate Field Defects Do Not Validate the Lamina Cribrosa as the Primary Site of Injury

Co-authored with Aziz Hasnain. Demonstrates that the lamina cribrosa cannot be the primary site of injury because no arcuate arrangement of nerve fibers exists within it.

Science Inquest · 2017

Chronic Glaucoma: A Two-Stage Disease

Proposes that chronic glaucoma occurs in two stages: a biological stage of border tissue degeneration, followed by a mechanical stage of lamina cribrosa sinking and nerve fiber severance.
